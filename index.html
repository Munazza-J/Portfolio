Hobbies

I often escape into books, sketch scenes from my imagination, and write stories—basically, I love creating things, whether it’s with words, lines, or code.
These things help me disconnect from the real world while giving me a way to express myself. I think that’s why coding calls to me—it’s another way to bring my ideas to life and share them with others.

✦ Reading ✦
I like to read books of various genres. This is a hobby that I picked back in school when my teachers used to encourage us to read as much as we could. I like to read Classics, High Fantasy, Historical Fiction, and Mystery. Reading is a way for me to live countless lives through different characters, understand the world through different minds, look at things through different points of view, and experience a lot of things that I might not be able to do in real life.
✦ Writing ✦
All the reading naturally inspired me to write my own stories. It started with diaries and journaling, turned into writing down ideas that I wouldn't work on, until recently when I found time to finally start writing. I've written a few poems so far, and I am working on a book as well. Writing really helps me express myself, share my imagination, dreams, and views, and give insight into my character, thoughts, and feelings.
✦ Drawing ✦
I sometimes draw, paint, or do digital art. It's a good way to show my artistic side, there are so many things everywhere that inspire me—the books I read, nature, life, and so on. I also draw a few scenes from the book that I am writing.
✦Threading✦
One of the many skills that I learned from school is needlework. I know how to embroider, stitch, and crochet. I stitch the most and I also learned how to knit but never really practiced that skill unlike the others. The feeling of wearing or using something that I made by myself is indescribable.
